Introduction

Global tempretures are rising and studies have found that humanity is the primary factor of this change.

In an attempt to combat climate change, world leaders assembled in Paris to open a dialog about the global effects of climate change and what each nation can do separately and collectively. Collectively, world leaders agreed upon a broad spectrum reduction of carbon emissions is paramount to success, setting the emission reduction goal by 2030. That substantial reinvestment in renewables and nuclear in both public, political and financial aspects would need primary focus of each member nation. However, methods each member nation will employ to develop and enact their own climate change policies would be left up to that nation member alone. World leaders also agreed to a 2℃ temperature change limit to negate the more extreme effects of climate change based upon forecast models based on the emission reduction goal by 2030.

Wait? Humanity is causing cimate change?

Sadly, yes. Periods of rapid population growth always showed correlation with rising levels in the consumption of commodities and the resources needed to support this greater level of consumption. With increased demand for consumer goods, increased demand is created for sustained energy. This sustained energy comes in the form of electric energy production by burning fossil fuels such as Coal, Natural Gas, and Petroleum producing excess amounts of carbon dioxide, the primary heat capturing greenhouse gas.

  • The Climate Today
  • Oxford Review of Climate Change Policy

How?

Buring fossil fuels as an energy source. When it was discovered that fossil fuels such as coal can be processed to generate cheap electric energy without understanding the carbon byproduct; this artificial carbon source disrupted the natural occurring greenhouse gas balance. Greenhouse gasses occur naturally in water, land and air. These gasses work by trapping recycled solar heat released from the planet’s crust. After these gasses absorb the heat, these gasses either pass the absorbed heat to other greenhouse gasses or redirects the heat back out to space. As the effects of climate change become more pronounced with the passage of time, Climate Scientists proven these higher than normal emission levels have begun to interfere with the natural functionality of the immense ecosystems around the globe.

Public Involvement and Private Sector Investment

For nuclear energy to flourish in the 21st century, public interest is paramount to success. Some methods to build public investment into nuclear energy:

  • Community and nuclear industry leaders working together, educating the public with current methods and information regarding nuclear energy to being rebuilding trust in the nuclear industry.

  • Dispelling long-held fears relating to reactor complex safety and maintenance in the light of the recent nuclear incidents like Fukushima Daiichi disaster.

    • Public Attitudes to Nuclear Power

    Some options the Private Sector can invest into general energy generation and nuclear power at the consumer and national level: :

  • Invest in consumer based energy efficiency technologies like “smart home” devices such as the Nest Thermostat or Lifx LED bulbs to exercise greater energy control.

  • Investment in energy conservation technology such as large battery storage for customer homes such as Powerwall developed by Tesla.

    • Tesla Powerwall Overview

    • Research and development investments into the next generation of nuclear power such as NuScale small modular reactors or Westinghouse’s AP1000 nuclear reactor with an cutting-edge passive safety system

    Local, State, and Federal Action

    What are State and Local Communities planning to do about climate change?

    Quite a lot. In the United States, communities have begun to work with local utility companies across the nation to promote energy efficiency action plans such as:

  • Home appliance recycling to incentivize upgrades to energy efficient appliances.

  • Cash rebate programs to incentivize energy use reduction during peak usage hours like free or reduce price “smart home” devices or utility price discounts.

  • Home improvement programs to incentivize building new residences focusing on energy efficient building construction

    • Community Energy Efficiency Resources

    States have started enacting action plans such as:

  • Installing renewable energy generation technology like solar panels on government buildings to supplement fossil fuel generated electricity.

  • Transit cash incentives in the form of fuel rebates for carpooling, reduced ride prices for mass transit during peak hours

  • Increased remote worker/telecommute positions to reduce transit to offices and other government sites

    • State Energy Efficiency Resources

    Federal Actions:

    This question is challenging to answer. Nuclear energy deployment would require considerable and highly progressive energy policies and the political willpower to follow through with such policies. Some steps the Government can take to advance the shift to clean energy from fossil fuels:

  • Introduce policy aiming to total recession of government subsidies catering to current energy production methods that use coal and natural gas as fuel sources to spur the shift to low-carbon energy production methods.

    • Federal Subsidies in Energy for 2016

  • Enact policy measures that calls for a mandatory federal carbon tax.

  • Draft policies mandating the use of carbon capture storage technologies on carbon heavy industries to help offset emission levels and focus development of energy efficiency standards.
